Frequently Asked Questions

  • Expungement gives you a clean slate. Having criminal charges on your record can prevent you from finding employment, public housing, and other government services. Expunging your criminal record can make accessing work, housing, and services easier.

    • The Harms of a Criminal Record
    • The Harms of a Criminal Record On Employment

  • If you do not know the exact charge, date, or outcome of your case, you can search your adult criminal record (including traffic violations) at the State of Maryland Judiciary Case Search. (Disclaimer: if your case was recently resolved, or currently pending, this site might not be updated with that information).

    If you have a juvenile record, you can contact the court that heard your case to get your record.

  • The answer to this question is a difficult one. There are many factors that determine if your record is expungeable. This difficulty is why we created ExpungeMaryland. By using this app you will be able to get to the bottom of whether or not your case is expungeable.

  • It costs $30 to expunge a single case. However, many people qualify for a fee waiver that makes expungement free. If you are eligible for expungement, your attorney can help you file for a fee waiver.

  • The statute says this process should take you 90 days. But in some jurisdictions, like Baltimore City, it can take up to 8 months.

  • ExpungeMaryland.com can only help you expunge your criminal record in Maryland. You’ll need to contact an attorney or legal aid service in the state where your criminal record is for more information.
